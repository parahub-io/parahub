Instructions:
This form is designed for deep self-analysis and reflection on your thoughts, feelings, and behavior. There are no "right" or "wrong" answers. The goal is to describe your inner world and how you interact with reality as honestly and thoroughly as possible.

Please take your time, don't rush. Write freely, providing examples from your life if it helps to better reveal your thoughts. You can answer all questions or choose those that seem most relevant to you. The more detailed your answers, the more complete picture can be formed.

Section 1: Perception of Self and the World
    1. How would you describe yourself? What do you consider to be your main strengths and weaknesses? Are there any qualities you would like to change? Why?
    2. Describe your typical mood. How often and why does it change? What events or thoughts have the strongest influence on your emotional state?
    3. How do you perceive the world around you? Do you consider it generally friendly, hostile, neutral, predictable, chaotic? Provide examples that confirm your perception.
    4. What does "success" and "failure" mean to you? Describe situations when you felt successful or when you experienced failure. What thoughts and feelings accompanied these situations? How did you explain to yourself the reasons for success or failure?
    5. Your beliefs: Do you have firm beliefs (about yourself, about people, about life) that are rarely questioned? Describe some of them. Have there been cases when life made you doubt these beliefs? How did you react to this?

Section 2: Thinking Process and Decision-Making
    1. Decision-making: Describe how you usually make decisions (from minor everyday ones to important life choices). Do you rely more on logic, intuition, emotions, or advice from others? How much time do you usually need? Do you tend to doubt yourself after making a decision? Provide examples.
    2. Response to uncertainty and risk: How do you feel in situations of uncertainty? Are you willing to take risks? In what situations yes, and in what situations no? Describe your thoughts and feelings when facing risk.
    3. Working with information: How do you search for and process information (for example, when studying a new topic or before making a decision)? Do you tend to trust certain sources more than others? How do you relate to information that contradicts your opinion?
    4. Planning and time: How do you usually plan your activities? Is it easy for you to stick to plans? Do you tend to procrastinate? How do you estimate the time needed to complete tasks? Do you often make mistakes in these estimates?
    5. Recurring thoughts: Do you have intrusive or frequently recurring thoughts, ideas, images, or worries? Describe their content and the situations in which they most often arise. How do you cope with them?

Section 3: Emotions and Reactions
    1. Strong emotions: Describe situations that trigger strong emotional reactions in you (anger, fear, joy, resentment, guilt, shame, etc.). How do you usually express these emotions? How do they affect your thoughts and actions?
    2. Criticism and feedback: How do you react to criticism or negative feedback? What thoughts and feelings arise in you? Does criticism help you or rather hurt/demotivate you?
    3. Response to stress: Describe your typical reaction to stressful situations or difficulties. What do you do to cope? What thoughts come to mind in such moments?
    4. Mistakes and failures: How do you relate to your own mistakes? Can you learn lessons from them? Or do they throw you off track for a long time? Describe your thoughts and feelings after making a mistake.

Section 4: Interaction with Other People
    1. Perception of others: How do you usually perceive the intentions and motives of other people? Do you tend to trust people or rather expect deception? Are there situations when you make quick conclusions about people? What are they based on?
    2. Comparison with others: Do you tend to compare yourself with other people (in terms of achievements, appearance, abilities)? How do these comparisons affect your self-esteem and mood?
    3. Conflicts: How do you behave in conflict situations? Do you strive to avoid them, compromise, or defend your position to the end? Describe a typical scenario.
    4. Opinion of others: How important is other people's opinion of you? Does it influence your decisions and behavior? In what situations especially?

Section 5: Peculiarities and Concerns
    1. "Oddities" of thinking: Have you noticed any peculiarities in your thinking that seem unusual or "strange" to you? For example, superstitions, rituals, specific ways of reasoning, unusual associations.
    2. Perfectionism: Is striving for perfection in everything characteristic of you? How does it manifest? Does it help or hinder you in life?
    3. Black-and-white thinking: Do you tend to see situations, people, or yourself only in black or white terms (all or nothing, perfect or terrible)? Provide examples.
    4. Predicting the future: Do you often try to predict the future? Do you tend to expect rather negative or positive outcomes? How often do your predictions usually come true?
    5. Focus on negative/positive: What do you usually focus more on in life – problems and shortcomings or opportunities and good moments? How does this affect your condition?
    6. What worries you? Are there any aspects of your thinking, emotions, or behavior that cause you concern, prevent you from living or achieving your goals? Describe them.

Section 6: Additional
    1. Is there anything else important about your thinking, feelings, or behavior that you would like to add and that was not covered in the previous questions?
