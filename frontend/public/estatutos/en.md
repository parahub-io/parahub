## CHAPTER I — **General Provisions**

#### Article 1 **Name, nature, scope, duration and legal framework**

1. The association, hereinafter referred to as the "Association", shall adopt the name "PARAHUB - ASSOCIAÇÃO".

2. The Association is a private-law non-profit association of international scope.

3. The Association is established for an indefinite period and shall be governed by these Articles of Association, by any internal regulations that may be approved, and by the applicable legal provisions.

#### Article 2 **Registered office**

1. The Association has its registered office at Rua das Regueiras 78, Podame, Monção, 4950-670, Portugal.

2. By resolution of the Board of Directors, delegations or other forms of representation may be established wherever deemed appropriate for the pursuit of the Association's purposes, in accordance with the applicable legal provisions and these Articles of Association.

#### Article 3 **Purpose**

The Association's mission and statutory purpose is to promote research, dissemination and support for the development of open-source software and of open protocols and standards aimed at a global, decentralised and interoperable information infrastructure, referred to within the scope of the Association's activity as "Parahub" (hereinafter the "System"), namely through the provision of consultancy services, the provision of digital and data services, including data processing, and the operation of websites using a search engine.

#### Article 4 **Aims of the Association**

1. In pursuing its statutory mission, the Association operates at national, European and international level, promoting cooperation, technological, scientific and social innovation, and the mobilisation of its human, technical and financial resources exclusively for the pursuit of its statutory aims. Accordingly, the Association shall seek, in particular, to foster the development of the following areas:

   a) Innovation, research, development, testing, documentation and technological capacity-building in decentralised information systems, federated networks and open digital infrastructures, including the System's software, protocols and standards;

   b) Promotion of open source, decentralisation, digital sovereignty, privacy protection, freedom of information, information security and the resilience of digital infrastructures;

   c) Prevention, preparedness and readiness in the field of digital security, continuity of essential services and the ethical and responsible use of information technologies, through decentralised systems integrated with advanced communication technologies;

   d) Development, promotion and provision of open, decentralised and federated digital infrastructures, including public repositories, application programming interfaces (APIs), monitoring tools, technology services and publicly accessible datasets;

   e) Strategic international cooperation with global partners in the field of digital infrastructures, open data, decentralised technologies and open source.

2. The Association shall pursue, in particular, the following aims:

   a) To promote direct cooperation and the development of efficient, fair and sustainable economic models, including multilateral barter and demand-driven user marketplaces, through the use of the System's principles and technologies;

   b) To support and promote the principles of open source, decentralisation, privacy protection, freedom of information, interoperable and peer-to-peer (P2P) networks, and individual digital sovereignty;

   c) To conduct research, develop and promote standards, models and solutions for decentralised digital identity, reputation, governance and electronic signature systems, based on the principles of transparency, cryptographic protection, community participation and voluntary membership;

   d) To organise and hold educational events, conferences, seminars, workshops and other knowledge dissemination initiatives concerning the System's principles, associated technologies, digital literacy, decentralisation and the secure use of cryptographic tools;

   e) To foster the creation, development and support of an international community of users, developers, researchers and other participants who adhere to the System's principles;

   f) To raise funding through grants, donations, membership fees and other lawful sources, exclusively for the pursuit of its statutory aims;

   g) To promote the development and implementation of ecologically sustainable practices and circular economy models through the use of the System's technologies and principles;

   h) To contribute to increased transparency, access to information of public interest and digital literacy, with a view to improving citizens' quality of life and developing innovative information services;

   i) To develop, research, pilot and promote decentralised digital identity and electronic signature systems based on the principles of the Web of Trust and public-key cryptography, in compliance with applicable legislation;

   j) To research, develop and promote open protocols, models and tools intended to improve mutual understanding, conscious communication and effective cooperation between people, with respect for confidentiality and users' control over their own data;

   k) To promote the creation, collection, processing and dissemination of free, open and publicly accessible geospatial data, including cartographic materials and orthophotomaps;

   l) To support the development of decentralised communication networks, shared mobility solutions, decentralised logistics networks, Internet of Things infrastructures and efficient resource monitoring systems;

   m) To research, develop and promote open and decentralised protocols for financial settlements, mechanisms of economic cooperation and alternative dispute resolution of a voluntary and non-jurisdictional nature, within the scope of the System, without engaging in financial intermediation;

   n) To promote the defence of individual digital rights and freedoms, including the right to privacy, freedom of expression and control over one's own data in the digital space.

3. In pursuit of its aims, the Association may, in particular:

   a) Carry out or promote the conduct of studies, analyses, opinions, projects, and scientific, technical or informational publications on any matters directly or indirectly related to its aims, including through the establishment of working groups or through third parties;

   b) Develop, test, document, maintain and distribute software, protocols, technical standards, technological tools and/or digital infrastructures associated with the System, as well as create and maintain public code and documentation repositories;

   c) Organise and hold public events, information campaigns, training sessions, forums and collaborative platforms, whether in person or digital;

   d) Establish and maintain strategic partnerships, protocols and cooperation agreements with public or private entities, whether national or foreign;

   e) Collect, process, temporarily store, publish and make available publicly accessible data, including geospatial data, in compliance with applicable legislation on data protection, information security and the rights of third parties;

   f) Carry out any other lawful activities that prove necessary, appropriate or instrumental to the pursuit of its statutory aims.

#### Article 5 **Participation in national and international legal entities**

1. The Association may participate in legal entities or national and international bodies whose purpose is related, complementary or instrumental to the pursuit of its statutory aims.

2. The Association may also acquire holdings in limited liability companies, as well as in companies governed by special legislation and in complementary groupings of undertakings, provided that such participation proves necessary, appropriate or useful for the pursuit of its statutory aims, is not speculative in nature and does not constitute the pursuit of profit-making activity as its main purpose.

3. The holdings referred to in the preceding paragraphs shall not confer on the Association the right to carry out activities reserved by law to regulated entities, nor shall they prejudice its associative and non-profit nature. Any income derived therefrom shall be exclusively allocated to the pursuit of its statutory aims, in accordance with the Civil Code.

## CHAPTER II — **Members**

#### Article 6 **General Provisions**

1. Any natural person of legal age, or any legal entity, whether national or foreign, who shares the Association's aims as set out in Article 4 and acknowledges and accepts these Articles of Association, may become a member of the Association.

2. Members that are legal entities shall be represented in the Association's governing bodies by the persons presiding over their respective management body, including, where applicable, the chair of the executive committee or the managing director, without prejudice to the possibility of delegation of powers in accordance with the law.

3. The admission of members shall be decided by the Board of Directors, in accordance with these Articles of Association. The General Assembly may approve general criteria or supplementary admission procedures.

4. Admission as a member is subject to the payment of membership fees, unless otherwise resolved by the General Assembly, which shall determine the amount, periodicity, payment conditions and any exemption or reduction arrangements, upon proposal by the Board of Directors.

5. Payment of membership fees, where applicable, is a condition for the full exercise of membership rights, namely the right to vote at the General Assembly, without prejudice to any exceptional arrangements for suspension, exemption or deferral approved by the General Assembly.

6. In addition to membership fees, the Association may fund its activities through grants, donations, voluntary contributions from members, income from activities compatible with its statutory aims and any other lawful sources permitted by law.

7. Membership does not confer any proprietary rights over the Association's assets, nor does it entitle members to the distribution of profits, surpluses or economic benefits, which, where they exist, shall be allocated in full to the pursuit of the statutory aims.

8. Loss of membership may occur through voluntary resignation, exclusion or other legally admissible grounds, in accordance with the provisions of these Articles of Association and any internal regulation approved by the General Assembly, with the member's right to be heard and to mount a defence always guaranteed.

9. The procedure for the admission of new members, as well as the conditions, amount and exemption or reduction arrangements for membership fees, and the procedure for the exclusion of members, shall be set out in an Internal Regulation on Admission and Exclusion of Members, to be approved by the General Assembly in accordance with these Articles of Association and the Civil Code.

#### Article 7 **Rights and duties of members**

1. The general rights of members, in accordance with the law, these Articles of Association and the applicable internal regulations, shall be as follows:

   a) To attend and vote at the General Assembly;

   b) To elect and be elected to the Association's governing bodies in accordance with the terms set out in these Articles of Association;

   c) To request the convening of the General Assembly of the Association, in accordance with these Articles of Association, as well as to submit proposals, participate in their discussion and express a position on the matters under discussion;

   d) To inform and be informed, in a complete, clear and accurate manner, about the Association's activities, its financial situation and its plans and projects;

   e) To use the services that may be organised for the benefit of members, as well as to enjoy, in general terms, the benefits arising from the Association, under the conditions that may be established;

   f) To submit any suggestions, proposals and contributions deemed appropriate for the pursuit of the Association's statutory aims;

   g) To benefit from the Association's institutional support in matters related to its statutory activity, under the terms and within the limits to be defined by the governing bodies.

2. The general duties of each member shall be as follows:

   a) To comply with the provisions of these Articles of Association, the applicable internal regulations and to abide by the resolutions of the governing bodies taken within the scope of their respective powers;

   b) To contribute actively, loyally and responsibly to the pursuit of the Association's statutory aims and to its prestige and good name;

   c) To pay the membership fees determined in accordance with the terms, amounts and conditions set by the General Assembly;

   d) To attend the General Assembly meetings of the Association;

   e) To perform, with due diligence, the associative offices to which they are elected or appointed and which they accept, as well as the tasks assigned to them and which they accept;

   f) Not to engage in acts that harm the Association's reputation, regular operation or assets;

   g) To cooperate in the activities promoted by the Association, namely through the mobilisation of knowledge, human resources or assets of the entities in which they hold positions, always with respect for the governance rules and legal duties applicable to those entities;

   h) To fulfil all other obligations applicable to them under these Articles of Association, internal regulations (where applicable) and by law.

#### Article 8 **Loss of membership and suspension of rights**

1. Members who meet any of the following conditions shall lose their membership:

   a) Without prejudice to the provisions of sub-paragraph b), members who, through serious or publicly significant conduct, undermine the fundamental values, purpose or aims of the Association, or who commit serious breaches of these Articles of Association, internal regulations or lawful resolutions of the governing bodies;

   b) Members who fail to pay membership fees due, in accordance with paragraphs 4 and 5 of this Article;

   c) Members who are declared insolvent, whenever such condition proves objectively detrimental to the pursuit of the Association's purpose and aims;

   d) Members who communicate their withdrawal through a written statement addressed to the Board of Directors.

2. Loss of membership under sub-paragraph a) of paragraph 1 of this Article shall be decided by the General Assembly, upon proposal by the Board of Directors, with the right of the member to be heard (should they wish to exercise that right) being guaranteed prior to the resolution being taken.

3. Loss of membership under sub-paragraph b) of paragraph 1 of this Article shall be decided by the Board of Directors, as an act of ordinary management falling within its own competence, and shall not be of a disciplinary nature.

4. Members with membership fees outstanding for more than thirty days after the respective due date shall be automatically suspended from exercising the rights set out in Article 7, namely the right to vote and to participate in the General Assembly.

5. Once the period set out in the preceding paragraph has elapsed, the Association shall notify the member in writing, including by email, to regularise their situation within an additional period of thirty days, failing which they shall be excluded. Exclusion shall not entitle the member to the reimbursement of membership fees already paid or any other contributions made to the Association's assets, without prejudice to the Association's right to demand payment of fees due and unpaid.

6. Loss of membership under sub-paragraphs a) and b) of paragraph 1 of this Article shall be communicated to the respective member by email.

7. A member who, for any reason, ceases to be a member shall have no rights over the Association's assets and shall not be entitled to the reimbursement of membership fees or any other contributions made, on whatever basis.

## CHAPTER III — **Administration and operation**

### Section I — **General Provisions**

#### Article 9 **Governing bodies of the Association**

1. The governing bodies of the Association shall be:

   a) The General Assembly;

   b) The Board of Directors;

   c) The Sole Auditor.

2. The term of office of the members of the General Assembly Bureau, the Board of Directors and the Sole Auditor shall be three years, with re-election permitted.

3. Elections shall be held on the basis of separate lists for each governing body, and each list must expressly identify the candidates and the specific office to be held by each of them.

4. The holding of offices in the Association's governing bodies shall, as a rule, be unremunerated. The General Assembly may resolve to grant remuneration to one or more members of the governing bodies, upon a reasoned proposal by the Board of Directors and in accordance with the law.

5. Upon expiry of the term of office, the members of the Association's governing bodies shall remain in office in a caretaker capacity until the election and inauguration of their successors, unless they are removed from office or resign in the meantime.

6. The Association's governing bodies, including the General Assembly, may meet and pass resolutions by means of electronic communication, provided that the identification of participants, the regularity of participation and the authenticity of resolutions are ensured. Resolutions may also be taken unanimously in writing, without a formal meeting, or at a meeting not preceded by the formalities prescribed in these Articles of Association, provided that, in either case, all members of the body in question expressly accept such form of resolution.

#### Article 10 **Working groups**

1. The Association may establish working groups, whether temporary or permanent, with functions of study, analysis, monitoring or technical support within the scope of the pursuit of its statutory aims. Working groups may be supported, in the exercise of their functions, by employees, specialists or technicians who are part of the Association's staff, as well as, where appropriate, by qualified external support, without prejudice to compliance with applicable legislation.

2. Working groups may analyse, study and formulate proposals or recommendations on any matters directly or indirectly related to the Association's aims, in accordance with their respective mandate and the guidelines set by the Board of Directors.

3. The establishment of working groups, the definition of their purpose, composition, duration, coordination, rules of operation and their respective dissolution shall fall within the exclusive competence of the Board of Directors, which may determine such elements by its own resolution or through internal regulation.

4. Working groups shall not constitute governing bodies of the Association, shall have neither deliberative powers nor powers of external representation, and shall perform their functions in a purely advisory capacity.

### Section II — **General Assembly**

#### Article 11 **Composition of the General Assembly**

The General Assembly is the sovereign body of the Association and is composed of all members in full enjoyment of their membership rights.

#### Article 12 **Powers of the General Assembly**

The General Assembly shall have the power to:

   a) Elect and remove at any time the Bureau, the Board of Directors and the Sole Auditor;

   b) Define the general guidelines for the Association's action;

   c) Consider and approve the annual report and accounts presented by the Board of Directors, after obtaining the opinion of the Sole Auditor;

   d) Interpret and amend the Articles of Association;

   e) Approve the Association's internal regulations;

   f) Resolve the dissolution of the Association and determine the manner of its liquidation;

   g) Establish the criteria for determining the membership fees to be paid by members;

   h) Resolve on the loss of membership in accordance with the provisions of Article 8;

   i) Resolve on any other matter of interest to the Association or to the persons it represents, provided it has been duly convened for that purpose.

#### Article 13 **General Assembly Bureau**

1. The General Assembly Bureau shall be composed of a Chair, a Vice-Chair and a Secretary.

2. The Chair shall be responsible for deciding on requests for the convening of the General Assembly and for convening it, as well as for presiding over General Assembly meetings and directing proceedings.

3. The Secretary shall be responsible for assisting the Chair in directing the proceedings of the General Assembly and for drawing up the minutes of meetings.

4. In the absence or temporary incapacity of the Chair, the Vice-Chair, in the first instance, and the Secretary, in the second instance, shall replace the Chair in the order indicated, namely in presiding over the General Assembly. In the event of the simultaneous absence of the Chair, the Vice-Chair and the Secretary, the General Assembly shall, exceptionally, appoint two (2) members to assume their functions at the said General Assembly.

#### Article 14 **Convening of the General Assembly**

1. The General Assembly shall meet in ordinary session by 31 March of each year to consider and vote on the Board of Directors' report and accounts and the Sole Auditor's opinion, and every three years to hold elections for the governing body offices. It shall also meet in extraordinary session whenever so requested in accordance with paragraph 2 of this Article.

2. The General Assembly shall meet in extraordinary session whenever convened by the Chair of the General Assembly Bureau on their own initiative or at the request of the Board of Directors, the Sole Auditor or a group of at least one third of the members.

3. The General Assembly shall be convened by notice posted at the registered office and by postal notice sent at least eight days in advance to each member, expressly indicating the venue, date and time of the meeting and the respective agenda, as well as, where applicable, the venue, date and time of the meeting on second call.

4. With respect to members who have given their prior consent, the General Assembly may, in lieu of postal notice, be convened by email with read receipt.

#### Article 15 **Proceedings of the General Assembly**

1. At the General Assembly, each member shall have the number of votes to which they are entitled.

2. A member may be represented at General Assemblies (whether meeting on first or second call) by another member, by means of a letter addressed to the Chair of the Bureau.

3. The General Assembly may not pass resolutions on first call without the attendance of more than half of the members entitled to vote. On second call, it may be validly constituted and pass resolutions with any number of members present or represented.

4. Without prejudice to cases in which a higher deliberative quorum is established by law or by these Articles of Association, resolutions of the General Assembly shall be taken by an absolute majority of the votes of the members present or represented, with abstentions not being counted.

5. Resolutions to amend the Articles of Association shall require the favourable vote of three quarters of the votes of the members present or represented, with abstentions not being counted.

6. The holding of the General Assembly and voting by means of electronic communication that ensure the proper identification of participants and the recording of their vote shall be permitted, under the terms to be set out in internal regulation.

### Section III — **Board of Directors**

#### Article 16 **Composition of the Board of Directors**

1. The Board of Directors is the management body of the Association, having competence to resolve on all matters not expressly assigned to other governing bodies.

2. The Board of Directors of the Association shall be composed of 3 or 5 members, elected solely from among members, being a Chair, a Vice-Chair and a Treasurer.

3. In the event of cessation of duties of a member of the Board of Directors, a new member may be co-opted to complete the current term of office, such co-optation being subject to ratification at the first General Assembly meeting that follows.

#### Article 17 **Powers of the Board of Directors**

1. The Board of Directors shall be responsible for representing, directing and managing the Association, carrying out everything that is necessary or expedient for the achievement of the Association's aims.

2. The Board of Directors shall, in particular, be responsible for:

   a) Representing the Association in and out of court;

   b) Implementing the resolutions of the General Assembly;

   c) Creating and organising all services, appointing and dismissing the respective staff;

   d) Defining and approving the Association's annual and multi-annual activity plans;

   e) Preparing the annual report on the Association's activities and presenting it, together with the accounts and the opinion of the Sole Auditor, for consideration and vote by the General Assembly;

   f) Admitting and excluding members;

   g) Drafting the Association's internal regulations for approval by the General Assembly;

   h) Proposing to the General Assembly the amounts of admission fees, ordinary or extraordinary membership fees to be borne by members as contributions to the Association's assets.

#### Article 18 **Proceedings of the Board of Directors**

1. The Board of Directors shall meet in ordinary session with such frequency as it deems necessary and in extraordinary session whenever convened by its Chair. It may validly operate provided that a majority of its members is present.

2. Resolutions shall be taken by a majority of votes of the members present or represented, provided that a majority of members is present or represented. The Chair shall have the casting vote.

3. Minutes signed by the members of the Board of Directors present shall be drawn up for each meeting.

#### Article 19 **Power to bind the Association**

1. The Association shall be bound by the signature of the Chair of the Board of Directors or, alternatively, of the Vice-Chair, the intervention of either one individually being sufficient.

2. For acts of routine administration, the intervention of any one member of the Board of Directors shall suffice.

### Section IV — **Sole Auditor**

#### Article 20 **Composition**

1. The oversight of the Association shall be exercised by a Sole Auditor. A deputy may also be elected, under the terms to be determined by the General Assembly.

2. The Sole Auditor and the Deputy Auditor shall be elected by the General Assembly in accordance with these Articles of Association.

3. The Deputy Auditor shall replace the Sole Auditor in the event of absence, temporary incapacity or vacancy of office.

#### Article 21 **Powers of the Sole Auditor**

The Sole Auditor is the oversight body of the Association, and shall be responsible, in particular, for:

   a) Monitoring and overseeing the administrative, financial and asset management acts of the Board of Directors;

   b) Providing the Board of Directors with any collaboration requested;

   c) Periodically verifying the regularity of the books, accounting records and supporting documents, as well as the existence of any assets or valuables belonging to the Association;

   d) Preparing an annual report on its oversight activity and issuing an opinion on the report, balance sheet, accounts and financial proposals submitted by the Board of Directors;

   e) Issuing a mandatory opinion on the dissolution and manner of liquidation of the Association;

   f) Ensuring strict compliance with the law, the Articles of Association and internal regulations.

## CHAPTER IV — **Financial Regime**

#### Article 22 **Assets**

1. The assets of the Association shall comprise:

   a) The initial and subsequent capital contributions made by the founding members or by any other members;

   b) The property, rights, valuables and resources acquired or obtained by the Association in the course of its activity, on any lawful basis;

   c) Donations, bequests, inheritances, grants and other contributions attributed to it, in accordance with the law;

   d) The results, surpluses or positive balances from its activities, where they exist.

2. Capital contributions made by members or third parties shall be definitively incorporated into the Association's assets, shall not confer any proprietary rights, shall not be reimbursable and shall under no circumstances give rise to claims for restitution or participation in results.

3. The Association's assets shall be exclusively liable for its obligations. Members shall not be personally or jointly liable for the Association's debts, except in cases expressly provided for by law.

4. The regime for the management, allocation, protection and possible disposal of the Association's assets may be the subject of supplementary regulation, to be approved by the General Assembly in accordance with these Articles of Association and the Civil Code.

#### Article 23 **Revenue**

1. The Association's revenue shall consist, in particular, of:

   a) Admission fees and membership fees, should they be established, in accordance with these Articles of Association and by resolution of the General Assembly;

   b) Income from the Association's own assets;

   c) Co-financing, grants, subsidies, donations or contributions awarded by any public or private entities, whether national or international;

   d) Income from activities lawfully permitted and consistent with the Association's statutory aims, provided that such activities do not contravene its non-profit nature;

   e) Any other lawful revenue.

2. Admission as a member may entail the payment of admission fees or membership fees, where these are established, under the terms, amounts and conditions set by the General Assembly.

#### Article 24 **Expenditure**

The Association's expenditure shall comprise all expenditure that proves necessary, appropriate or expedient for the pursuit of its statutory aims and for the regular operation of the Association.

---

## CHAPTER V — **Final Provisions**

#### Article 25 **Dissolution**

1. The dissolution of the Association may only be resolved at a General Assembly specially convened for that purpose, by the favourable vote of three quarters of all members.

2. In the event of dissolution, liquidation shall be carried out in accordance with the terms established by the General Assembly, without prejudice to applicable legislation.

#### Article 26 **Gaps and omissions**

In all matters on which these Articles of Association are silent, the general regime governing associations as set out in the Civil Code and other applicable legislation shall apply.
